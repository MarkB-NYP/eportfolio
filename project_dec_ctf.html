Project Scope

For the 2021 December holidays, Nanyang Polytechnic's InfoSecurity club (NYP InfoSec) organized our own Capture-The-Flag (CTF) competition. CTFs are a fun and exciting way to practice our various cybersecurity skills, from coding to cryptography.

As the club's Head of Development, I came up with event's scenario and theme, as well as coordinated the creation of a few key challenges.

My Role And Solution

Since the event would be held at the end of the year, I created a fictional storyline where a hacker group known as EternalWinter takes control of a weather-control machine and starts sending threats to different governments. I pitched the scenario to the committee members, who were all enthusiastic about the idea.

The shared scenario helped the committee brainstorm possible challenge ideas and helped all our challenges appear part of a bigger mission.

My Work Process

First, I designed a document with information about the scenario as well as some custom that graphics I created. I shared this with everyone working on the event so we would all be on the same page.

During the challenge creation process, I collaborated with several skilled committee members to develop a special set of cross-discipline challenges. Drawing on each of our interests, these challenges included analyzing a PDF file for a hidden program, hacking into a web server and taking control of a virtual network.

Finally, I supervised the creation of the competition landing page (as seen in the video above). This vibrant website used plenty of web design tricks and helped us get a few more last-minute sign-ups.

Outcome and Results

In total, the club was able to create more than 50 challenges for the event. We had close to 60 participants over the two-day event, with an overwhelming majority of participants mentioning
that they would like to attend a similar event in the future.
Many also appreciated the scenario’s story and design.
