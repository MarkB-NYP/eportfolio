Project Scope

In late 2021, Nanyang Polytechnic's InfoSecurity Club (known as NYP InfoSec) had nearly a hundred members, most of whom were fellow students in IT-related Diplomas. The Executive Committee members agreed that it was time expand further, perhaps to the rest of the school. A key step would be setting up a public website.

Our main goal was for a website that would introduce the club, highlight our events, and encourage new members to sign up. Eventually, we hoped that a visible online presence would also help us form meaningful partnerships with other like-minded organizations.

My Role And Solution

At the time, I was the club's Head of Development. I volunteered to oversee the creation, launch, and initial upkeep of the website.
Since I would be starting from scratch. While working out code and content seemed a daunting task, the blank canvas turned out to be a great opportunity for me to recommend and implement several innovate ideas.

After exploring several web hosting options, I recommended building our website with Google's Blogger Content Management System. While it was less customizable than certain other options, Blogger was extremely easy to set up and run. Future committee members would also be able to update the website quickly. Most importantly, it was free, which meant no strain on our budget.

My Work Process

Since I had used Blogger a few times before, I was able to quickly configure the website’s themes and layout. Then, I wrote most of the main content and gathered any missing information from other committee members. When I was satisfied with the website, I gave the other committee members access so they would also be able to make posts.

For some extra content, I came up with the idea of a special page with links to the club’s partners. I shared this idea with other tech-related clubs in the school and they appreciated the idea. Some even offered to do the same on their own websites!

A major obstacle during development was that the website templates from Blogger’s didn’t always scale well on smaller screen sizes, such as on a mobile screen. Fortunately, I knew enough coding to fix the templates.

Outcome and Results

We launched the website in December 2021, where it received more than 500 total views in the following month.

I continue to supervise a new post to the website for each event we organize. This helps us sustain participant engagement and build our reputation. These posts also give our publicity team new content to post on social media.
